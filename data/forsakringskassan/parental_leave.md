# Försäkringskassan — Parental Leave (Föräldrapenning)

## Source
- URL: https://www.forsakringskassan.se/english/parents/when-the-child-is-born/parental-benefit
- Agency: Försäkringskassan (Swedish Social Insurance Agency)
- Last Updated: 2026-03-22

## Overview

Sweden has one of the most generous parental leave systems in the world. Parents share 480 days of paid parental leave per child. The benefit is called föräldrapenning (parental benefit) and is administered by Försäkringskassan.

## Eligibility

You are eligible for parental benefit if you:
- Are the parent of the child (biological, adoptive, or partner)
- Are registered in Sweden (have a personnummer)
- Have been living in Sweden (or working for a Swedish employer)
- Are insured in Sweden under EU regulations or bilateral agreements

## How Parental Leave Works

### Total Days
- **480 days** per child (shared between both parents)
- **240 days** are reserved for each parent
- **90 days** are non-transferable (each parent must use their own 90 days)
- **150 days** can be transferred to the other parent

### Payment Levels
- **390 days** at sick pay level (sjukpenningnivå):
  - ~80% of your salary, up to a ceiling of ~SEK 1,218/day (2026)
  - Minimum ~SEK 250/day if you have low or no income
- **90 days** at basic level (grundnivå):
  - ~SEK 180/day

### When Can You Use It?
- From 60 days before the due date
- Until the child turns 12 years old
- Can be used full-time, half-time, quarter-time, or one-eighth time

## Steps to Apply for Parental Leave

1. **Report the birth** — The hospital reports the birth to Skatteverket
2. **Log in to Försäkringskassan** — Use BankID at forsakringskassan.se
3. **Plan your leave** — Decide how many days and at what level
4. **Notify your employer** — At least 2 months before planned leave
5. **Apply for parental benefit** online:
   - Select the dates you want to take leave
   - Choose payment level (sjukpenning or grundnivå)
   - Submit application
6. **Receive payment** — Processed monthly, payment around the 25th

## Important Notes

- You can take parental leave at the same time as your partner (double days)
- Self-employed parents are also eligible (based on SGI — income qualification)
- If you move to Sweden with a child under 12, you may still get remaining days
- Employers often top up the benefit to near 100% of salary (check your employment agreement)
- You can take leave in non-consecutive periods
- "Temporary parental benefit" (tillfällig föräldrapenning) is for caring for a sick child — 120 days/year per child
