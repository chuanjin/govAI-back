# Försäkringskassan — Student Benefits (Studiestöd)

## Source
- URL: https://www.csn.se/languages/english.html
- Agency: CSN (Swedish Board for Student Finance) / Försäkringskassan
- Last Updated: 2026-03-22

## Overview

Students in Sweden can receive financial support for their studies. The Swedish student aid system consists of a study grant (bidragsdel) and a study loan (lånedel). This is administered by CSN (Centrala studiestödsnämnden). Additionally, students may be eligible for housing allowance from Försäkringskassan.

## Study Grant and Loan (Studiestöd)

### Eligibility
- Swedish citizens, EU/EEA citizens living and studying in Sweden
- Non-EU citizens with permanent residence permit
- Must be enrolled in approved educational programs
- Must study at least half-time (50%)
- Age limit: generally up to 60 years old

### Monthly Amounts (2026)
- **Study grant** (free money): ~SEK 4,000/month
- **Study loan** (to be repaid): ~SEK 8,000/month
- **Total maximum**: ~SEK 12,000/month

### Steps to Apply
1. **Get admitted** to a study program in Sweden
2. **Create an account** at csn.se
3. **Apply online** — Submit application for each semester
4. **Provide required information**:
   - Personal number
   - Proof of enrollment
   - Bank account details
5. **Receive decision** — Usually within 2-4 weeks
6. **Funds disbursed** — Quarterly payments to your bank account

## Housing Allowance for Students

### Eligibility (from Försäkringskassan)
- Age 18-28 without children
- Age 18+ with children
- Have housing costs
- Have low income

### Steps to Apply
1. **Log in** to forsakringskassan.se with BankID
2. **Apply for bostadsbidrag** (housing allowance)
3. **Report your income** and housing costs
4. **Maximum amount**:
   - Without children: up to ~SEK 1,300/month
   - With children: up to ~SEK 5,900/month (depending on family size)

## Important Notes

- Study grant does NOT need to be repaid — it's free money
- Study loan must be repaid after graduation (income-based repayment)
- You must pass your courses — failing may reduce or stop your aid
- If you work while studying, your income may affect your aid (income ceiling applies)
- International students from outside EU/EEA generally pay tuition fees
- Swedish citizens studying abroad can also receive CSN aid
