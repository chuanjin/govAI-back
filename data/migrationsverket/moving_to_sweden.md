# Migrationsverket — Moving to Sweden Checklist

## Source
- URL: https://www.migrationsverket.se/English/Private-individuals.html
- Agency: Migrationsverket (Swedish Migration Agency)
- Last Updated: 2026-03-22

## Overview

Moving to Sweden involves several steps with different government agencies. This guide helps you understand the overall process, whether you're an EU citizen or coming from outside the EU.

## First Steps When Moving to Sweden

### For EU/EEA Citizens

1. **You have the right of residence** — No permit needed for the first 3 months
2. **Register with Skatteverket** (Tax Agency) — If staying more than 1 year
3. **Get a personal number** (personnummer) — Essential for everything
4. **Open a bank account** — Bring ID and proof of personal number
5. **Register with Försäkringskassan** — For social insurance benefits
6. **Find housing** — Register for apartment queues or search privately
7. **Get a Swedish ID card** — Apply at Skatteverket after getting personnummer

### For Non-EU/EEA Citizens

1. **Apply for residence permit** BEFORE moving to Sweden
2. **Wait for decision** — Do not travel before getting your permit
3. **Pick up residence permit card** at Swedish embassy
4. **Arrive in Sweden** — Bring all original documents
5. **Register with Skatteverket** (Tax Agency) — Visit in person
6. **Get a personal number** (personnummer)
7. **Register with Försäkringskassan**
8. **Register with Arbetsförmedlingen** if looking for work
9. **Open a bank account**
10. **Find housing**

## Key Agencies You'll Interact With

| Agency | Swedish Name | Purpose |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Tax Agency | Skatteverket | Personal number, tax, population register |
| Migration Agency | Migrationsverket | Residence permits, citizenship |
| Social Insurance | Försäkringskassan | Benefits, parental leave, sick leave |
| Employment Service | Arbetsförmedlingen | Job seeking, integration programs |
| Health Insurance | Region (Landsting) | Healthcare, doctor visits |

## Important Tips

- Get your personal number as FIRST priority — most services require it
- Many services are available online with BankID (digital identification)
- Swedish is the main language, but many services are available in English
- Join your municipality's introduction program if you're a new immigrant
- Healthcare is subsidized — register at your nearest vårdcentral (health center)
- Swedish courses (SFI) are free for immigrants
