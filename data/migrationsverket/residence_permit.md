# Migrationsverket — Residence Permit (Uppehållstillstånd)

## Source
- URL: https://www.migrationsverket.se/English/Private-individuals.html
- Agency: Migrationsverket (Swedish Migration Agency)
- Last Updated: 2026-03-22

## Overview

Non-EU/EEA citizens need a residence permit to live and work in Sweden for more than 90 days. There are different types of permits depending on your reason for moving: work, family reunification, studies, or asylum.

## Types of Residence Permits

### Work Permit (Arbetstillstånd)
- For non-EU/EEA citizens with a job offer in Sweden
- Employer must advertise the position in Sweden/EU first
- Valid for up to 2 years, renewable
- Tied to the specific employer for the first 2 years

### Family Reunification (Anhöriginvandring)
- For family members of someone living in Sweden
- Spouse/partner, children under 18
- The person in Sweden must be able to support the family

### Student Residence Permit (Uppehållstillstånd för studier)
- For students admitted to a Swedish university
- Must prove full-time studies
- Must have health insurance
- Must prove financial means (SEK 10,314/month for 2026)

### Asylum (Asyl)
- For those fleeing persecution or war
- Apply at Migrationsverket upon arrival in Sweden

## Steps to Apply for a Work Permit

1. **Get a job offer** from a Swedish employer
2. **Employer posts the position** — Must be advertised for 10 days in Sweden/EU
3. **Employer creates a case** in Migrationsverket's system
4. **You apply online** at migrationsverket.se
5. **Required documents**:
   - Valid passport
   - Job offer/employment contract
   - Proof of salary (minimum 80% of Swedish collective agreement)
   - Health insurance and life insurance from employer
   - Photo (passport format)
6. **Pay application fee** — SEK 2,000
7. **Biometrics** — Visit Swedish embassy/consulate for fingerprints and photo
8. **Wait for decision** — Processing time varies (1-12 months)
9. **Receive residence permit card** — Pick up at embassy or in Sweden

## Steps for Family Reunification

1. **Family member in Sweden applies** (reference person)
2. **You apply online** from your home country
3. **Required documents**:
   - Valid passport for all family members
   - Marriage certificate (apostilled)
   - Birth certificates for children
   - Proof of housing in Sweden
   - Proof of income (reference person)
4. **Interview** at Swedish embassy/consulate
5. **Wait for decision** — Can take 6-21 months
6. **Receive residence permit card**

## Important Notes

- EU/EEA citizens have right of residence — they don't need a permit (just register with Skatteverket)
- Processing times vary significantly — check current times at migrationsverket.se
- You can track your case online on migrationsverket.se
- A work permit does NOT automatically include family members — they must apply separately
- After 4 years of continuous residence, you may apply for permanent residence
- Permanent residence requires meeting income and language criteria (from June 2026)
