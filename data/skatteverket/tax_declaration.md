# Skatteverket — Tax Declaration (Deklaration)

## Source
- URL: https://www.skatteverket.se/english
- Agency: Skatteverket (Swedish Tax Agency)
- Last Updated: 2026-03-22

## Overview

Everyone who earns income in Sweden must declare their taxes annually. The Swedish Tax Agency sends out a pre-filled tax return (inkomstdeklaration) every spring. Most people can approve it digitally. Self-employed individuals and business owners have additional obligations.

## Who Must Declare Taxes

- Anyone who earned income in Sweden during the tax year
- Anyone who received Swedish pension
- Anyone who owned property or made capital gains
- Self-employed individuals and freelancers
- Anyone who received rental income

## Employees (Anställda)

### Steps to Declare as an Employee

1. **Wait for your tax return** — Arrives in March/April (digitally or by post)
2. **Review the pre-filled information** — Check salary, deductions, employer contributions
3. **Add any deductions** — Travel to work (over 11,000 SEK), home office (if eligible)
4. **Approve your declaration** — Deadline is usually May 2nd
5. **Ways to approve**:
   - BankID (digital signature) — fastest
   - SMS — if you have registered your phone
   - Paper — sign and mail the form
   - Phone — call the IVR service
6. **Receive your tax assessment** — Usually in June-August
7. **Tax refund or payment** — If you overpaid, you get a refund. If underpaid, you must pay

## Freelancers and Self-Employed (Egenföretagare)

### Steps to Declare as a Freelancer

1. **Register for F-skatt (self-employment tax)** — Do this when you start your business
2. **Keep accounting records** — All income and expenses throughout the year
3. **Pay preliminary tax monthly** — Based on estimated annual income
4. **Fill in NE appendix** — Business income and expenses
5. **Submit declaration** — Same deadline (May 2nd), but more complex form
6. **VAT reporting** — If turnover exceeds 80,000 SEK/year, register for VAT

### Deductions for Self-Employed
- Office space costs
- Equipment and tools
- Travel expenses for business
- Internet and phone (business portion)
- Professional development

## Important Notes

- Late filing results in a penalty fee of 1,250 SEK (first time), can increase
- You can request an extension in special circumstances
- If you disagree with your assessment, you can appeal within 6 years
- Digital filing is recommended — faster processing and refund
- Foreign income must also be declared (Sweden has tax treaties with many countries)

## Tax Rates (2026)

- Municipal tax: approximately 32% (varies by municipality)
- National tax: 20% on income above 598,500 SEK
- Capital gains: 30%
